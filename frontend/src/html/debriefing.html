University of Chicago Debriefing Statement

Study Number: IRB20-1281

Study Title: Creative Perceptions

Researcher(s): Isaac Cohen, Youngjae Cha, Shigehiro Oishi

Thank you for your participation. We would now like to tell you a little more about the study.

Purpose of the Study:

This study examines whether people who believe that future labor markets will increasingly be dominated by the “creative class” are less likely to seek advice from older adults. The proposed mechanism is that stronger creative-class beliefs may lead individuals to place less value on accumulated experience and domain expertise, which in turn may reduce the perceived value of older adults as sources of guidance. To test this, participants will first report their beliefs about the future importance of creative occupations, and then evaluate a series of adult faces varying in age. Using a between-subjects design, participants will rate either their willingness to seek practical advice, emotional advice, or perceived intelligence from each target. This design allows us to test whether creative-class beliefs specifically predict reduced advice-seeking toward older adults in domains associated with expertise, while ruling out alternative explanations related to general warmth or emotional support.

We use a novel artificial face judgment technique (adapted from Alex Todorov) to generate faces that vary in age but are consistant in perceived attractiveness and happiness. We expect that participants who are more innovative, believe that the future will be dominated by the creative class, and embody creative ideals in their lifestyle and jobs will tend to seek out less professional advice from older faces.

As this study is ongoing, we ask that you not discuss this study with anyone else who is currently participating or might participate at a future time.
